Raspberry PI Server & PIC32MM

The board on the left is a Raspberry Pi Zero W. Using a Linux-based operating system, I configured it to act as a portable server. Although the board itself is quite tiny, I am able to use adapters to connect 4 terabytes of storage and easily access the space through a simple Wi-Fi connection. The board on the right is a PIC32MM Curiosity Development Board. Although no practical uses have been implemented, I configured the board to alternate between 4 LED states using the 2 switches, interrupts, and an integrated 555 timer.
