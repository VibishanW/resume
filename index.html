Introduction

FPGA/embedded engineer with an M.S. in ECE from Northeastern and a B.S. in EECS from UMass Lowell. I work on mission-critical FPGA systems across RF/SDR, high-speed datapaths, and firmware/software integration.

I have broad expertise with humble beginnings as a purely backend software engineer developing & maintaining mission planning software before pivoting careers to firmware/hardware. I've had a strong desire to build a multi-faceted understanding of technology from the bud of my career. I decided to double major in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science during my undergrad. This decision was driven by the urge to build a strong foundational base in everything, from how electricity works to the complex use cases driven by application-level algorithms today. After starting my professional career in software, I felt that while my technical abilities improved, my research centered development had dwindled. As a result, I accepted a Masters opportunity at Northeastern University to 1. quench my thirst for deeper knowledge and 2. explore the research side of engineering through the lens of a thesis. Thanks to a strong support network of family, friends, and advisors I successfully completed my master's part-time while continuing my career full-time. Motivated by my broadened knowledge and with the support of my company I shifted to a FPGA/Firmware position midway through my degree to work more closely with hardware. Having completed my master's at the end of 2025 I successfully published a thesis exploring hardware-accelerated human pose estimation on AMD Versal AI Engines (VCK5000).

My journey as an engineer is quite unique featuring a lot of twists and turns. My engineering mentality is heavily influenced by an avante-garde approach to learning computer science outlined by George Hotz in a document labeled "From the Transistor to the Web Browser". In this document, Hotz tears down flaws in modern computer science curriculum and introduces a more radical approach.

Always open to opportunities and happy to connect so feel free to message me anytime!
